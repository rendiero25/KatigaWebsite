# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Indonesian consumers (primarily 18–35) shopping for KumaKuma branded products through katiga.id. They browse on mobile while commuting or at home, already decided to buy, and want a fast frictionless path from cart to payment. Occasional desktop users completing larger orders.

## Product Purpose

Full-stack e-commerce + company profile site for katiga.id. Lets customers discover, browse, and purchase KumaKuma products. The CMS allows the team to manage content, orders, and catalog without code. Success = order placed, customer returns.

## Brand Personality

Playful, Friendly, Approachable. Warm and confident — a homegrown Indonesian brand with genuine product quality. Not corporate, not sterile. Speaks like a person, not a policy.

## Anti-references

- Tokopedia/Shopee: too much visual noise, badges everywhere, racing-green discount banners
- Generic flat corporate e-commerce: no character, interchangeable with any white-label store
- Heavy gradients everywhere: feels dated, try-hard, early-2010s
- Dense forms with tiny text: overwhelming, unreadable on mobile, kills trust at the last mile

## Design Principles

1. **Warmth over polish** — a touch of warmth (off-white backgrounds, friendly copy) beats cold clinical white every time for this brand
2. **Clarity at the last mile** — the cart and checkout are where trust is won or lost; every element must earn its place, nothing should create doubt
3. **Mobile first, desktop natural** — layout decisions start from the phone screen; desktop gets more breathing room, not a different experience
4. **Consistent, not repetitive** — reuse the emerging design system (border tokens, radius, typography) but vary rhythm and spacing across sections
5. **Buttons do one thing** — every button has one clear job; no text-link action buttons anywhere in the purchase flow

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA target. Sufficient color contrast on all text. Focus states visible. Interactive elements minimum 44×44px touch target on mobile. No motion-only affordances.
